Yummy



Yummy is a Shudder-exclusive horror film directed by Lars Damoiseaux. It was released in 2019 and tells the story of a young couple who journey to a hole-in-the-wall clinic for a medical procedure. At the clinic, they discover that illegal experimentation has been taking place, and has resulted in the creation of zombies.

Yummy is one of those films that can be described as a bit mean-spirited at times. It is a horror comedy but one with sharp teeth. This is not a film that you’d want to watch around Mom and Dad. The jokes can be crass, and it often tight-walks along the line between decent and lewd.

None of the characters have redeeming qualities.

Michael, the protagonist, played by Bart Hollanders, is about as decisive as a dead fish. He sort of comes across as a dead fish for most of the film. He has a phobia of blood, which makes him projectile vomit and faint. Just what you need in a zombie crisis.

His girlfriend Alison, played by Maaike Neuville, is slightly more likable but it is hard to take her seriously with the hentai-proportion breasts she has been given in the film (they go to the clinic so that she can have a breast reduction). The other characters are a rag-tag bunch of unlikables from a selfish travel agent with a drug addiction to a ruthless clinic assistant with a nefarious agenda.

You aren’t exactly crying when any of them gets the chop.

In case the names of the actors didn’t give it away, Yummy is a Dutch film with English spoken sporadically throughout. So, if subtitles aren’t your thing, maybe give this one a miss. Also, if the off-the-wall sentiments of Dutch films aren’t your thing, give this one a miss too.

The special effects, as is the case for most Shudder-exclusive films, are really good. Most if not all of them are practical. I wouldn’t eat when watching this film. Zombie films are by genre disgusting. And Yummy is no exception. A few scenes had me grimacing and one scene in particular where a zombie consumes itself had me turning slightly green.

High-brow, it is not, but Yummy does have its place in the genre. It is less serious than the Walking Dead and meaner than Shawn of the Dead. It is definitely not genre defying like 28 Days Later. But it is a fun watch on a Friday night. It does try its best to subvert the genre wherever possible in its own weird way.

I would have preferred if there had been one less sex joke and not as many close-ups of women’s scantily clad bodies. I think if a few of those scenes had been scrapped, I would have enjoyed Yummy a lot more, and it wouldn’t have teetered on the drop just before the Exploitation film cesspool.

Am I going to recommend this to everyone I know? No. Do I regret watching it was a glass of wine on a Friday night? No.



Final thoughts:

  • Rating: Five out of ten brains
  • Would not watch again
